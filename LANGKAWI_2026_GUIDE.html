The Island

Welcome to Langkawi

An archipelago of 99 islands off the northwest coast of Malaysia, Langkawi is a UNESCO Global Geopark blessed with limestone mountains, turquoise waters, pristine beaches, and rich rainforest — all wrapped in duty-free paradise status.

Why Pelangi Resort is the Perfect Base

Pelangi Beach Resort & Spa sits directly on Pantai Cenang — Langkawi's most vibrant beach strip — giving you beachfront access alongside a constellation of cafés, restaurants, and casual bars within walking distance. Its central position means Day 1 is effortlessly relaxed and Day 2 excursions are quick to reach by car or Grab.

2-Day Plan

Your Langkawi Itinerary

A perfectly paced two-day plan: Day 1 for settling into the island rhythm, Day 2 for Langkawi's most iconic scenic adventure.

01
Day One
Beach, Sunset & Island Vibes
Pantai Cenang
🌅
Morning · 9am–12pm
Arrival & Resort Settle-in
Check into Pelangi Resort, have a slow breakfast, and take your first dip. Don't rush — let the island pace wash over you. The beach is right there.
🍽️
Lunch · 12pm–1:30pm
Casual Cenang Lunch
Head along the Pantai Cenang strip for a laid-back lunch. Try FatCUPID for local fusion, or grab nasi lemak from a roadside warung to keep it authentically Langkawi.
🏖️
Afternoon · 2pm–5pm
Beach Walk & Cenang Strip
Stroll the beachfront, dip into duty-free shops along Cenang Road, or head 10 minutes south to the quieter Pantai Tengah for a more serene stretch of sand.
🌇
Sunset · 6pm–7pm
Golden Hour at Pantai Cenang
Return to the beachfront for drinks as the sun drops over the Andaman Sea. The Cliff Restaurant offers extraordinary sunset views over the water — book ahead if you want a table at the edge.
🌙
Evening · 7:30pm–9:30pm
Night Market or Seafood Dinner
If it's Thursday, the Temoyong night market in Pantai Cenang is a must — over 50 stalls of ikan bakar, nasi lemak, satay, and fresh tropical fruit. Otherwise, head to Orkid Ria for outstanding fresh seafood in an atmospheric setting with hanging lanterns.
🍹
Optional · 9:30pm+
Night Cap at Cenang
Since Langkawi is duty-free, drinks are refreshingly affordable. Several beachside bars stay open late along the strip — keep it easy for Day 2's early start.
Pro tip: Day 1 is intentionally relaxed. Resist the urge to cram in sightseeing — you'll enjoy Day 2's adventure far more with a rested body and a genuine feel for the island's pace.
02
Day Two
SkyCab, SkyBridge & Mountain Views
Oriental Village
🌄
Early Morning · 8am–9am
Early Start — Beat the Crowd
Leave Pelangi by 8am for Oriental Village (~20 min drive). Morning gives you the best light, fewer queues at the SkyCab, and cooler temperatures for the bridge walk.
🚡
Morning · 9am–11am
Langkawi SkyCab to the Top
Board the SkyCab — one of the world's steepest cable cars — rising to 708m above sea level at Machinchang mountain. The views over the turquoise Andaman Sea and surrounding islands begin well before the summit.
🌉
Late Morning · 11am–12pm
Sky Bridge Walk
Walk the 125-metre curved suspension bridge hanging above dense jungle — the world's longest curve suspension bridge. The 360° panoramic views of sea, islands, and rainforest are genuinely breathtaking.
🍜
Midday · 12pm–2pm
Lunch at Oriental Village
The lakeside Oriental Village complex has several café and restaurant options. Rest your legs, browse the colourful shops blending Malay, Chinese, and Japanese architecture, and recover from the altitude with cold drinks.
💧
Optional · 2pm–3:30pm
Telaga Tujuh (Seven Wells)
Just 10 minutes from Oriental Village, the Seven Wells Waterfall cascades down natural limestone pools on Machinchang's slopes. A short jungle walk rewards you with a beautiful natural swimming spot.
🌊
Afternoon · 4pm–6pm
Back to Pelangi — Pool & Spa
Return to Pelangi by 4pm with plenty of daylight for one last pool session, spa treatment, or a peaceful final beach walk before packing up.
🦞
Final Evening · 7pm+
Farewell Sunset Dinner
Round off the trip with a proper beachside dinner — either at Pelangi's own restaurant or at Pia's Padi for scenic paddy field views and outstanding local cuisine. Toast to a great escape.
Swap option: If you prefer water over mountains, island hopping to Dayang Bunting Lake + snorkeling in the Andaman is an equally spectacular alternative for Day 2. Book tours the evening before from Cenang Jetty.
Things To Do

Top Attractions

Beyond the 2-day itinerary — everything worth knowing if you have extra time or want to swap an activity.

🚡
Langkawi SkyBridge & SkyCab
Scenic Landmark

The world's longest curved suspension bridge at 125m, reached via one of the world's steepest cable cars rising to 708m. Panoramic views of the Andaman Sea, limestone peaks, and surrounding islands. Visit early morning for clear skies and shorter queues.

Must-Do ~2 hrs Book ahead
🛥️
Island Hopping Tour
Marine Adventure

Speedboat tours visiting 3–4 islands over a full day. Key stops: Dayang Bunting (hidden freshwater lake inside a limestone island), Pulau Singa Besar wildlife reserve, and snorkelling over shallow reefs. Life jackets and snorkel gear included. Departs from Cenang Jetty.

Half-day or Full-day ~RM 35–60/pax
🌿
Kilim Karst Geoforest
UNESCO Geopark

Southeast Asia's first geopark — a river canyon of limestone karst cliffs draped in mangroves. Boat tours glide through bat caves, eagle-watching spots (Brahminy Kite & White-bellied Sea Eagle), and floating fish farms. A genuinely Jurassic Park atmosphere.

2–3 hrs Wildlife ~RM 270 private boat
🐧
Underwater World Langkawi
Aquarium

One of Southeast Asia's largest aquariums — featuring sharks, rays, and marine life in a walk-through underwater tunnel, plus a Sub-Antarctic penguin section and vibrant coral displays. Great for an afternoon cool-down, especially for families.

~2 hrs Family-friendly ~RM 30–40/pax
🦅
Eagle Square (Dataran Lang)
Landmark

Langkawi's iconic 12-metre eagle statue overlooks the bay near Kuah Jetty. A worthwhile quick stop for photos, and the surrounding area has great views of the harbour and easy access to Kuah Town for shopping and cheap local food.

30 mins Free Near Kuah Town
💧
Telaga Tujuh Waterfall
Nature

Seven cascading natural pools on Machinchang's slopes, reached by a short jungle hike from the car park. The name means "Seven Wells" — a cool, peaceful swim in nature. Pairs perfectly with the SkyCab visit as it's just 10 minutes away.

1–2 hrs Swimming Near Oriental Village
🎨
3D Art Museum
Entertainment

Over 200 hand-painted optical illusion murals across multiple themed rooms — you step into the artwork for immersive, shareable photos. Located at the SkyCab complex, making it easy to combine. A fun 1–2 hour diversion.

1–2 hrs At Oriental Village
🏖️
Tanjung Rhu Beach
Beach

Often rated Malaysia's most beautiful beach — a pristine white sand crescent at the island's northern tip with virtually no crowds, crystal-clear water, and dramatic views of limestone outcrops. A 40-minute drive from Pantai Cenang but absolutely worth it for a half-day escape.

Half-day Secluded North of island

Eat & Drink

Food & Dining

Langkawi's food scene blends Malay, Indian, Chinese, and Western influences. Seafood is the star — fresh, affordable, and best eaten by the sea.

🦞
Orkid Ria Seafood
Seafood · Pantai Cenang

The go-to for fresh seafood in Cenang. Hanging Chinese lanterns, aquariums of live lobster and grouper, and outstanding black pepper tiger prawns, chilli crab, and cheese-baked lobster. Order by weight — the prices are fair for the quality.

RM 40–120/pax
🌅
The Cliff Restaurant
Malay-Western Fusion · Pantai Cenang

Perched above the beach with sweeping Andaman Sea views. The setting is the selling point — sunsets here are extraordinary. The menu covers Malay and Western fusion; cocktails are duty-free priced. Book a table at the edge for golden hour.

RM 30–80/pax
🌾
Pia's The Padi
Local Malay · Near Cenang

Run by chef Pia and her husband Jimmy, this charming restaurant serves outstanding local cuisine against scenic paddy field views. Great vegetarian options alongside classic Malay dishes. Beloved by both locals and long-stay visitors.

RM 20–50/pax
🍔
FatCUPID
Local Fusion · Pantai Tengah

A relaxed favourite along Pantai Tengah offering everything from nyonya laksa and beef rendang to salmon fishcakes and steak sandwiches. Fresh ingredients, generous portions, and a laid-back vibe. Perfect for a casual lunch.

RM 15–45/pax
🐟
Wonderland Food Store
Seafood · Kuah Town

A no-frills waterfront seafood spot in Kuah that locals rate highly. Seafood is priced by size and weight with the day's freshest catch on display. A great option if you're stopping at Eagle Square — Kuah Town is right next door.

RM 25–70/pax
🥗
The Fat Frog
Healthy & Vegetarian · Golf Course

Perched atop a golf course with lush green views, this café serves a health-focused menu popular with vegetarians and active travellers. Carefully crafted homely dishes in a scenic setting — a great brunch or light lunch option on Day 2's return.

RM 20–45/pax

Good to Know

Practical Tips

Everything you need to know before you arrive — transport, money, weather, and how to make the most of your 2 days.

🚗
Getting Around
  • Langkawi has no public transport — rent a car (RM 70–120/day) for the most freedom
  • Grab works in Pantai Cenang and the airport, but drivers can be scarce in remote areas
  • Taxis are unmetered — negotiate fixed fares upfront or use Grab for transparent pricing
  • Scooters are fun but Langkawi roads get slippery in rain — exercise caution
  • Oriental Village is ~20 min drive from Pantai Cenang — easy by car or Grab
💰
Money & Duty-Free
  • Currency: Malaysian Ringgit (RM). ATMs available at Kuah Town and Cenang area
  • Langkawi is duty-free — alcohol, chocolate, and cigarettes are significantly cheaper than mainland Malaysia
  • Night markets and street food are cash-only — carry small bills (RM 5–20)
  • Most restaurants and resorts accept cards; markets and hawker stalls prefer cash
  • Budget roughly RM 80–150/day for food and activities beyond accommodation
☀️
Weather & When to Go
  • Best season: Nov–April — dry skies, calm seas, ideal for all activities
  • Peak: December–February — busiest and most expensive, book ahead
  • Shoulder: March–April and Oct–Nov — good weather, fewer crowds
  • Monsoon: May–October — rain is frequent but usually short bursts, not all-day
  • Temperature stays ~30°C year-round; humidity is high — light breathable clothing is key
🎟️
Booking & Planning
  • Book SkyCab tickets online in advance — queues can be very long during peak months
  • Island hopping tours can sell out — book the day before at your hotel or Cenang Jetty
  • Mangrove/Kilim tours can be booked via WhatsApp — most operators reply within hours
  • Pelangi Resort restaurants are popular for sunset dinners — reserve your table early
  • Check night market schedule against your exact dates before planning Day 1 evening
🎒
What to Pack
  • Light, breathable clothes — cotton or linen; humidity is constant
  • Reef-safe sunscreen — very important for outdoor time and snorkelling
  • Water shoes or sandals with grip — useful for waterfalls and mangrove jetties
  • Mosquito repellent — especially for evening outdoor dining or nature areas
  • Dry bag or ziplock for boat trips — keep your phone safe during island hopping
  • Light rain jacket — brief tropical showers can appear even in dry season
🕌
Culture & Etiquette
  • Langkawi is predominantly Muslim — dress modestly when visiting towns and markets
  • Beachwear is fine at Pantai Cenang, but cover up when leaving the beach area
  • Remove footwear before entering mosques or private homes
  • Friday afternoons see many local businesses close for prayers
  • Tipping is not expected but always appreciated — RM 5–10 for exceptional service
